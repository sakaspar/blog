The skincare industry is a racket.

They sell you a 10-step routine that costs $500 and takes 45 minutes every morning. They use words like "infused" and "botanical" to distract you from the fact that most of the ingredients are just cheap fillers.

I had terrible skin for years. I tried the expensive stuff. It didn't work.

Then I stopped listening to the marketing and started listening to the science. I built a routine for under $30 that cleared my skin in 21 days.

Here it is.

---

## The $30 Budget Breakdown

1. **Cleanser :** CeraVe Foaming Facial Cleanser (The big bottle) : **$12**
2. **Moisturizer :** The Ordinary Natural Moisturizing Factors + HA : **$6**
3. **Sunscreen :** Trader Joe's Daily Facial Sunscreen (SPF 40) : **$9**
4. **Active :** The Ordinary Niacinamide 10% + Zinc 1% : **$6** (Wait, that's $33? Close enough. Buy them on sale).

---

## The Morning Routine (3 Minutes)

1. **Splash with water.** You don't always need to cleanse in the morning if you did it at night. Don't strip your natural oils.
2. **Niacinamide.** This is the "magic" ingredient for texture and pores.
3. **Moisturize.** Lock in the hydration.
4. **Sunscreen.** **Mandatory.** If you don't wear sunscreen, everything else is a waste of time. UV damage is the #1 cause of skin issues.

---

## The Evening Routine (5 Minutes)

1. **Cleanse.** Get the dirt, oil, and sunscreen off your face.
2. **Active.** (Optional: Retinol if you're over 25. Otherwise, stick to Niacinamide).
3. **Moisturize.** Use a slightly thicker layer at night.

---

## The Golden Rules

- **Don't touch your face.** Your hands are covered in bacteria.
- **Change your pillowcase.** Every 3 days. Seriously.
- **Drink water.** Your skin is an organ. It needs internal hydration.
- **Consistency > Intensity.** Doing a simple routine every day is 100x better than doing a complex one once a week.

---

## The Result

In three weeks, the redness was gone. The texture smoothed out. The breakouts stopped.

I didn't need a "medical-grade" serum. I didn't need a vibrating face brush. I just needed to stop irritating my skin and start protecting it.

Stop overpaying. Start simplifying. :
