No gym. No dumbbells. No excuses.

Everything on this list can be done in your bedroom, your living room, or a 2x2 meter patch of floor. I've tested all of it. This is the routine I actually use.

---

## Why bodyweight training works

People underestimate calisthenics because there's no weight machine involved. That's the point. You're moving your own body through space — building functional strength, coordination, and endurance all at once. No monthly fee. No commute. No waiting for equipment.

The only thing you need is consistency.

---

## The Routine

### 1. Pike Push-Up — 50 reps
*Target: shoulders, triceps, upper chest*

Get into a push-up position, then walk your feet toward your hands until your hips are high in the air — your body forms an upside-down V. From there, bend your elbows and lower your head toward the floor, then push back up.

This is the bodyweight equivalent of an overhead press. 50 reps will light your shoulders up.

![Pike push-up](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1598971457999-ca4ef48a9a71?w=700)

---

### 2. Shoulder Taps — 50 reps (25 each side)
*Target: shoulders, core stability, balance*

Start in a high plank position — arms straight, body in a straight line. Lift your right hand and tap your left shoulder. Put it back. Lift your left hand and tap your right shoulder. That's two reps.

Keep your hips as still as possible. The instinct is to sway side to side — resist it. That's where the core work happens.

![Shoulder taps plank](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1566241440091-ec10de8db2e1?w=700)

---

### 3. Push-Ups — 50 reps
*Target: chest, triceps, shoulders, core*

The classic. Hands slightly wider than shoulder-width, body straight from head to heels, chest touches the floor on the way down. No half reps.

If 50 straight is too much to start, break it into sets: 20 + 15 + 15. The goal is 50 total with good form.

![Push-ups](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1616803689943-5601631c7fec?w=700)

---

### 4. Diamond Push-Ups — 20 reps
*Target: triceps, inner chest*

Same as a push-up, but bring your hands together under your chest with your thumbs and index fingers touching — forming a diamond shape. Keep your elbows tight to your body as you lower down.

These are harder than they look. Your triceps will feel it immediately.

---

### 5. Bodyweight Squats — 50 reps
*Target: quads, glutes, hamstrings*

Feet shoulder-width apart, toes slightly out. Sit back and down like you're sitting into a chair, keeping your chest up and knees tracking over your toes. Drive through your heels to stand back up.

50 reps with full range of motion — below parallel every time.

![Bodyweight squat](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1574680096145-d05b474e2155?w=700)

---

### 6. Jump Squats — 20 reps
*Target: explosive power, quads, glutes, cardio*

Same squat pattern, but at the top of the movement you explode off the ground. Land softly, absorb with your knees, go straight into the next one.

These spike your heart rate fast. If you're doing this circuit with minimal rest, jump squats are where you'll feel the cardio hit.

---

### 7. Lunges — 40 reps (20 each leg)
*Target: quads, glutes, hamstrings, balance*

Step forward with one leg, lower your back knee toward the floor, then push back to standing. Alternate legs. Keep your front knee behind your toes and your torso upright.

For an extra challenge: add a pause at the bottom for 1 second each rep.

![Lunges](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1597452485669-2c7bb5fef90d?w=700)

---

### 8. Plank Hold — 3 x 1 minute
*Target: core, shoulders, lower back*

Forearms on the ground, body straight, hips level. Hold.

One minute feels long when you're doing it right. If your lower back is sagging or your hips are in the air, you're cheating the rep. Reset and hold the correct position.

![Plank](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1559486017-4d63e1a8b4f7?w=700)

---

### 9. Mountain Climbers — 50 reps (25 each leg)
*Target: core, hip flexors, cardio*

High plank position. Drive one knee toward your chest, then quickly switch legs — like you're running horizontally. Keep your hips down and your core tight.

Go at a controlled pace rather than sprinting through them. Form over speed.

---

### 10. Burpees — 15 reps
*Target: full body, cardio, conditioning*

Stand → squat down → jump feet back to plank → push-up → jump feet forward → jump up with arms overhead. That's one rep.

15 burpees at the end of this circuit will finish the job. They're not fun. That's the point.

![Burpees](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1593079831268-3381b0db4a77?w=700)

## How to structure it

**Beginner:** Do each exercise, rest 60–90 seconds between, rest 2 minutes between rounds. Start with 1 round.

**Intermediate:** Rest 30–45 seconds between exercises. 2 rounds total.

**Advanced:** Minimal rest (15–20 seconds). 3 rounds. Time yourself and try to beat it next session.

## The only rule

Show up. The workout is easy to understand and hard to do consistently. That gap — between knowing and doing — is where most people stay.

Close it.
