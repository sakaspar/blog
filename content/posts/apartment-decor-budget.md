Instagram would have you believe that a nice apartment requires a $10,000 IKEA haul and a West Elm addiction.

They're wrong.

I just moved into a new studio. I had exactly $150 in the "decor" budget. I wanted it to look like a high-end Airbnb, not a college dorm.

Here is exactly how I spent every dollar.

---

## The $150 Breakdown

| Item | Source | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Large Area Rug | Facebook Marketplace | $30 |
| Floating Shelves (4) | DIY (Scrap wood + brackets) | $20 |
| Indoor Plants (6) | Propagations + Clearance | $15 |
| Floor Lamp | Thrift Store | $10 |
| Velvet Pillow Covers | Amazon | $15 |
| Wall Art | Digital Downloads + Thrifted Frames | $25 |
| Lighting (Smart bulbs) | Sale | $20 |
| **Total** | | **$135** |

---

## The Strategy : How to Win at Thrifting

### 1. Facebook Marketplace is the King
If you buy furniture new, you are paying a "convenience tax". Most people just want their old stuff gone. I found a $300 rug for $30 because the owner was moving that afternoon.

**Pro Tip :** Search for "moving sale" or "must go today".

### 2. The Power of "Greenery"
Plants make a space feel alive and expensive. I didn't buy full-grown fiddle leaf figs. I went to the "death row" section of Home Depot and bought the 50% off plants that just needed water. I also asked friends for cuttings.

### 3. Lighting > Furniture
You can have the most expensive couch in the world, but if you have harsh overhead fluorescent lighting, your apartment will look cheap. I bought two warm-toned floor lamps and some smart bulbs.

Controlled lighting creates **mood**. Mood creates **value**.

---

## The DIY Hacks

- **The Gallery Wall :** I didn't buy art. I bought $1 digital downloads from Etsy and printed them at a local shop. I put them in mismatched frames I found at Goodwill and spray-painted them all black. Unified look for $25.
- **The "High-End" Smell :** Don't buy $50 candles. Buy an essential oil diffuser. It lasts longer and looks cleaner.

---

## The Result

My friends came over and asked who my designer was. When I told them the total was under $150, they didn't believe me.

Design isn't about how much you spend. It's about **intent**. It's about choosing a color palette and sticking to it. It's about finding the "hero" pieces (the rug, the art) and letting everything else be simple.

You don't need more money. You need more **curation**. :
