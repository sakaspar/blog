The internet is obsessed with morning routines.

Wake up at 4 AM. Drink lemon water. Meditate for 30 minutes. Journal for 20. Cold plunge. Run 10km. By the time you're done with your "routine", you've spent 3 hours and you haven't actually **started** your day.

I tried all of that. It made me tired. It made me feel like a failure when I missed a step.

So I stopped. I built a routine that works for people who actually have work to do.

---

## Step 1 : Don't touch the phone (The 60-Minute Rule)

This is the hardest part. And the most important.

When you check your phone first thing in the morning, you are letting the world dictate your mood. Emails. News. Social media. You are reacting to other people's agendas before you've even set your own.

**Rule :** No phone for the first 60 minutes. Keep it in another room.

---

## Step 2 : Sunlight and Movement (The Biology Hack)

Your body needs light to wake up. Not the blue light from your screen : the actual sun.

I spend 5 minutes outside. I don't run. I don't do a full workout. I just walk. I look at the sky. I let my circadian rhythm know that the day has started.

- **5 minutes of light.**
- **5 minutes of movement.**
- **Zero equipment.**

---

## Step 3 : Eat the Frog (The Real Work)

Most people use their "routine" to procrastinate on the work they're afraid of.

I do the exact opposite. Once I've had my light and movement, I sit down and do the hardest task on my list. No coffee first. No breakfast first. Just **work**.

I do 90 minutes of deep work before the rest of the world is even awake. By 9 AM, I've already won the day. Everything else is a bonus.

---

## Step 4 : The Delayed Caffeine

If you drink coffee the second you wake up, you're masking your natural cortisol spike.

I wait at least 90 minutes after waking up before I have my first cup. This prevents the mid-afternoon crash that most people accept as "normal". It's not normal. It's a timing issue.

---

## Why this works

It's not about being a "morning person". It's about **protecting your focus**.

- **No phone** protects your brain.
- **Sunlight** protects your sleep.
- **Deep work** protects your career.
- **Delayed caffeine** protects your energy.

It's simple. It's boring. It's extremely effective.
