Social media is a drug. We know this. We've seen the documentaries. We've felt the phantom vibrations in our pockets.

I decided to go cold turkey. 90 days. No Instagram. No X. No LinkedIn. No TikTok. I didn't even keep the apps on my phone "just in case".

I thought I would suddenly become a zen monk. I was wrong.

Here is what actually happened.

---

## Week 1 : The Withdrawal

The first 7 days were brutal. I realized how often I use my phone as a shield.

- Waiting for an elevator? **Phone.**
- Awkward silence in a conversation? **Phone.**
- Commercial break? **Phone.**

Without the apps, I had to sit with my own thoughts. It was boring. It was irritating. I felt disconnected and "behind". I was constantly worried I was missing some vital piece of news or a joke everyone else was in on.

---

## Month 1 : The Deep Boredom

By week 3, the twitch to check my phone stopped. But then the boredom set in.

Real life is slower than the internet. Conversations don't have jump cuts. Books take time to read. Problems don't get solved in a 60-second clip.

I had to re-learn how to focus. My attention span had been shredded by years of scrolling. I couldn't read more than 5 pages of a book without my brain screaming for a notification.

**The Fix :** I started carrying a physical notebook. Whenever I felt the urge to scroll, I wrote a sentence. Any sentence. It gave my hands something to do.

---

## Month 2 : The Clarity

This is when the "benefits" finally showed up.

1. **Comparison died :** I stopped comparing my "behind-the-scenes" to everyone else's "highlight reel". I didn't know what my acquaintances were eating for lunch, and I realized I didn't care.
2. **Time expanded :** I "gained" about 3 hours a day. That's 21 hours a week. I used it to learn a new programming language and actually finish my workout routines.
3. **Conversations changed :** When I met friends, I actually had things to tell them because they hadn't seen my life on my Story already. And I actually listened to them.

---

## Month 3 : The New Normal

By day 90, I didn't want to go back.

The "FOMO" was gone. I realized that 99% of what happens on social media is noise. It's people shouting into a void, hoping for a "like" to validate their existence.

I eventually re-downloaded the apps, but the relationship has changed.

- **No notifications.** (The most important rule).
- **Time limits.** (30 minutes a day total).
- **Follow only people who provide value.** (If they just post selfies or complaints, they're gone).

---

## The Hard Truth

Quitting social media won't fix your life. But it will show you exactly what's wrong with it.

It removes the distraction. It forces you to look at the reality of your situation. And once you see it, you can't unsee it.

The screen is a thief. Take back your time. :
